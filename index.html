The Numbers That Matter

₹10L cr
Tamil Nadu's total debt doubled in 5 years — from ₹5.13 lakh crore in 2021 to nearly ₹10 lakh crore in 2026. Debt added in these 5 years alone is more than all debt Tamil Nadu accumulated in its first 60 years of existence.
22.8p
Out of every ₹100 the government earns, ₹22.80 goes only to pay interest on past loans — before a single teacher, doctor, road, or scheme gets funded. Karnataka pays ₹16.40. Maharashtra pays ₹10.30.
₹78K cr
Revenue deficit in 2025-26 is the highest ever recorded — higher even than the COVID year of 2020-21. The government is spending ₹78,324 crore more than it earns, every single year, just on day-to-day running costs.
5.45%
Tamil Nadu's tax collection relative to its economy is at an all-time low — despite having India's 2nd largest economy. Karnataka and Gujarat collect more GST in absolute terms, even though Tamil Nadu's economy is bigger.
1.44%
Capital expenditure — money spent on roads, hospitals, schools — fell to its lowest among comparable States. Gujarat spends 2.86% of its economy on building things. Tamil Nadu spends only 1.44%.
